Intramural Sports Replaced with "CMS Squad Training Tournament"

Linza Malcon | Aug. 10, 2019, 3:14 a.m.

In a much contested move, CMS Athletics has now shut down its intramural program entirely in favor of a “Squad Training Tournament” that will run once a semester. This decision comes in the wake of weeks of heated discussions and slow decline in club sport participation. Proponents of the Tournament have argued that participation should be mandatory, while defendants of the previous intramural leagues lament the loss of students’ remaining humanity.

“We’re barely any better than the monsters we’re fighting if we can’t have any fun anymore. I personally will be continuing to play soccer anywhere the Tournament isn’t happening every Wednesday at 8,” said one CMC student. “Anyone who wants to is welcome to join me, the bid fee for individuals is three bottles of water, but if you and 4 friends want to register as a team the team bid is only 12 bottles.”

Soccer is joined by inner tube water polo as one of the last remaining intramurals with highly vocal supporters. A senior from Mudd’s Case dorm (last year’s ITWP’s defending champions) shared some thoughts on the installation of the Tournament:

“CMS Athletics has spit in the face of the students it claims to care so much about. It would be one thing if they just dissolved the league, but they’ve gone farther than that. The open pool hours policy is draconian and specifically forbids the use of outside equipment. The tubes are going to be be repurposed into barricades for their stupid tournament! Who builds barricades out of inflatable tubes? They don’t want us to be able to play at all!”

The policies CMS is implementing do minimize options that might compete with the tournament. There has been a 5C-wide push towards using resources effectively, and many have commented that club sports had become a resource sink that only a few students are still benefitting from.

“Most students quit club sports ages ago,” said a Scripps sophomore. “The people who are still playing don’t seem to have noticed there’s a war going on. Like, zombies, literal zombies are screwing up stuff all over the country, and yet some people are still concerned about their pool games.”

Other students have expressed their enthusiasm for the upcoming Tournament. One CMC student shared their thoughts:

“It’s gonna be awesome!! Honestly if they’d put together a nerf tournament even before the whole zombie thing started, I think plenty of people would have been all over that. Like Mudd has a club or something for that shit already and they’re STILL complaining that inner tube water polo is getting shut down.”

Some Mudders do also support the new Tournament: “I think this falls right in line with the updates to the clinic program,” said a Mudd senior. “I mean, they’re calling it “Impact Work” now and everything. We need to win this fight and playing games is not the way to do so.”

Whether or not they have the full support of the student body, CMS Athletics has made the call to end club sports. Registration for the first Squad Training Tournament is now open and details of the rules can be found on the CMS website.
