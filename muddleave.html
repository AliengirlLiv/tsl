Why Mudd Should Leave

Amalia McKeen | Mar. 06, 2019, 10:22 a.m.

The past 3 months have have been marked by unprecedented cooperation among the Claremont Colleges. While the social structures have broken down around us on all sides, each of the other colleges has stepped up to protect the Consortium. Mudd, which has rejected other colleges' defense programs in favor of its own absurd robotics program, is the lone exception.

This obsession with theoretical schemes rather than practical solutions is not confined only to Mudd's overly ambitious Engineering department. Mudd's Impact squads, for example, spend almost as much time modeling combat simulations as they spend on weapons training.

Even Prof. Hindley, a widespread anti-robot advocate on Mudd's campus, seems to have fallen prey to the same tendency to pursue exploratory research over tangible preparation. Two Claremont Independent reporters found a discarded grant proposal from his biomedical research team in a wastebasket after a 5-C Council meeting. The research team, which claimed to be studying "Bio-medical weaponry crucial to our ongoing survival," submitted a proposal containing ridiculous line-items such as $10,000 to "purchase equipment to help our lab emulate the physiology of homo zombius". I hope that this proposal was promptly rejected as ridiculous, but even the existence of this proposal shows how deluded Mudd has become.

It is clear that this failure to address reality is endemic to Harvey Mudd's culture. The college has yet to contribute meaningfully to our collective defense and are slowly draining our limited food supply. If Mudders refuse to even consider the possibility that the colleges would be safer without their absurd research programs, perhaps the rest of the Claremont Colleges should consider the possibility that they would be safer without Mudd.
