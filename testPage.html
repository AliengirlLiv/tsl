Outside Agitators Interrupt Islamophobia Talk at Scripps

Corinne Mitchner Jaimie Ding | Oct. 13, 2017, 1:14 a.m.

Late Saturday night, Professor Hindley of Harvey Mudd was arrested in South Pomona by members of Pomona's LifeForce. After just two days of hearings, during which the bewildered professor gave no satisfying account for his actions, he was punished with permanent exile from the 5Cs.

When the joint 5-C council published the Ninety-Three Edicts, most assumed the draconian document was purely a scare tactic to curb the increasing frequency of incidents involving citizens of one college disregarding the orders of another college's LifeForce. This is the first incident in which anyone has been prosecuted for a Category 5 punishable offense.

A routine nighttime patrol by Pomona's Lifeforce, the Forty-Seventh, caught Prof. Hindley wandering through the Pomona forest at 3:00 A.M. on Saturday. According to reports, when the was confronted by the Forty-Seventh squad he dropped his shovel and tried to escape, only to immediately trip over the netting of the nearby baseball cage.

He was immediately arrested for violation of Curfew Edict VI.iii and placed under house arrest. Several members of the Forty-Seventh squad which arrested him commented on his distant expression. "I don't think it fully registered with him how much trouble he was in," remembered one LifeForce trooper. "While we handcuffed him he just kept muttering about his research."

At his trial the next day, Prof. Hindley refused to explain his presence in the forest last night. He refused to speak, even when the CMC government major standing in as prosecutor interpreted his silence as an indicator of guilt. She went so far as to suggest that that Prof. Hindley could have been aiding the zombies. Members of the Mudd community protested the accusations, pointing out that Prof. Hindley was a very private person at the best of times.

The gridlocked Pomona jury failed to reach a consensus on Sunday, but the next day members of the Semper Pomona Party took advantage of the Pomona Progress Party's absence from the court due to morning classes to form an alliance with the Pomona Agrarian party to convict the poor professor. Before lunchtime he had been sentenced to exile into the zombie-infested wastescape surrounding the colleges.

It was a heartbreaking moment by the Southern barricade this morning: a line of students craning their necks to see well-loved professor as he shuffled in the center of a circle of Forty-Seventh members. Noisy protests were banned at the event (Assembling Edict XX.ix), but many silently held signs with affectionate phrases like "We Love You Hindley." The silence was broken only by Prof. Hindley's student research team, who tried unsuccessfully to push through the LifeForce ranks to have one last word with their mentor and friend. We all are sobered by the severity of this punishment for such a minor offense. At this stage, we hold no illusions that exile is anything more than a death sentence - or more likely an undeath sentence.
